### Lesson 6: Mastering Light with Potentiometers

**What is a "Potentiometer"?**
Think of a Potentiometer (or "Pot") like a water faucet. A switch can only turn the water "on" or "off." A faucet lets you turn the handle to decide exactly how much water comes out. A Potentiometer lets you turn a knob to decide exactly how much electricity flows through your circuit—making your light dimmer or brighter!

---

### Activity 1: The "Dimmer Switch"

- **Components Needed**:
  - 9V Battery
  - Breadboard
  - Standard LED (1)
  - Potentiometer (1)
  - Resistor (1)
  - Jumper Wires
- **Objective**: Build a circuit where you can control the brightness of a single LED using a knob.
- **Activity**:
  1. Connect your LED and resistor to the breadboard.
  2. Add a Potentiometer. Connect the two outer pins to your Battery (Power and Ground) and the middle pin to your LED.
  3. **Simulation Challenge**:
     - Start the simulation.
     - Slowly turn the knob on the Potentiometer.
     - **Observation**: Watch how the LED brightness changes as you turn the knob.
  4. **Documentation**: In your Engineer's Notebook, describe how the knob acts like a "gate" that lets more or less electricity through.

### Activity 2: The "Rainbow Mixer"

- **Components Needed**:
  - 9V Battery
  - Breadboard
  - RGB LED (1)
  - Potentiometers (3)
  - Resistors (3)
  - Jumper Wires
- **Objective**: Use three Potentiometers to control the brightness of the Red, Green, and Blue pins on an RGB LED.
- **Activity**:
  1. Set up your RGB LED on the breadboard.
  2. Connect **three separate Potentiometers**, one for each color (Red, Green, Blue).
  3. As you turn each knob, observe how the color of the LED changes.
  4. **The Challenge**: Can you create the color purple? How about yellow? Turn the knobs to different positions to explore the entire color spectrum!
  5. **Documentation**: Draw the positions of the three knobs in your notebook when you find your favorite color.

---

### Activity 3: The Color Spectrum Discovery

- **Components Needed**:
  - 9V Battery
  - Breadboard
  - RGB LED (1)
  - Potentiometers (3)
  - Resistors (3)
  - Jumper Wires
- **Objective**: Use the Potentiometers to map the entire color range.
- **Activity**:
  1. Systematically turn one knob at a time while leaving the others at zero.
  2. Then, try turning two knobs halfway.
  3. Finally, try turning all three knobs to different spots.
  4. **Discovery**: What happens when all three colors are at their brightest? Does the light look white?
  5. **Final Notebook Entry**: Write down your "recipe" (e.g., Red: 10%, Green: 50%, Blue: 90%) for the most interesting color you discovered today.
