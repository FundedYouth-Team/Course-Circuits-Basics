# Circuits Basics

An introductory course on Electricity and Circuits. Targeted age 3rd-grade. Taught using TinkerCAD.com/circuits. A free tool. The goal of this course is to learn how a circuit works and how to do basic calculations to build the circuit.

## Lesson Breakdown

### [Lesson 1: Complete Paths & Switches](Lesson-01.md)

Students learn that electricity needs a complete loop to flow. Using the Tinkercad Circuits workspace, they build a battery-and-bulb circuit, see what happens when the loop is broken (an open circuit), and add a push button that acts as a "bridge" to open and close the path.

- **Step 0 — Using the Workspace**: Navigating the Tinkercad Circuits workspace.
- **Step 1 — The Always-On Loop**: Building a 9V battery and light bulb into a complete (closed) circuit.
- **Step 2 — Breaking the Path**: Seeing how an open circuit stops the flow of electricity.
- **Step 3 — The "Magic Bridge"**: Adding a push button that closes the path only when pressed.

### [Lesson 2: The Safety Guard (Resistors)](Lesson-02.md)

Students meet the LED and discover it can "pop" when connected straight to a 9V battery. They add a resistor to protect it, adjust the resistor's value to control brightness, and learn to read the Ohm (Ω) shorthand for prefixes like kilo, milli, and micro.

- **Water Pipe Analogy**: Picturing a resistor as a "pipe sizer" that sets a safe flow speed.
- **Step 1 — The "Pop"**: Why an LED breaks when wired directly to a 9V battery.
- **Step 2 — Introducing the Resistor**: Adding a resistor in-line to protect the LED.
- **Step 3 — Tinkering with Brightness**: Changing the resistor's Ohm value to dim or brighten the LED.
- **Step 4 — Decoding the Shorthand**: Reading unit prefixes (kilo, mega, milli, micro) and the Ohm value table.

### [Lesson 3: The Breadboard & Ohm's Law](Lesson-03.md)

Students move their circuit onto a breadboard and dig into the "why" behind resistors. Through voltage-drop ("toll booth") and current ("flow") analogies, they learn Ohm's Law and use it across three hands-on experiments to calculate the right resistor for single and series-chained LEDs.

- **Step 1 — Rebuilding and The Breadboard**: Recreating the Lesson 2 circuit and moving onto a breadboard.
- **Step 2 — Breadboard Assembly**: Placing components into the breadboard grid and wiring the power rails.
- **Step 3 — The Battery's "Toll Booths"**: Voltage drop — how each LED "takes" 2V (the waterfall analogy).
- **Step 4 — Picking the Right Resistor**: The "Goldilocks" math for soaking up leftover voltage.
- **Step 5 — Finding the "Flow"**: Current/amps, the "Three-Jump Rule," and reading a datasheet.
- **Step 6 — Ohm's Law**: Using R = V ÷ I across three experiments (single LED, 3-LED series, multi-color chain).
