# Circuit Explorer Series: Lesson 1

## Objective

By the end of this lesson, the student will be able to:

1. Navigate the Tinkercad Circuits workspace.
2. Explain that electricity must have a "complete path" (loop) to work.
3. Demonstrate how a button acts as a bridge to open or close that path.

### Step 1: The Always-On Loop

- **Concept**: Electricity requires a "complete path" (a closed loop) to travel from the battery, through the component, and back to the battery.
- **Activity**:
  1. Place a 9V battery and a light bulb in the workspace.
  2. Connect the Red (Positive) wire to Terminal 1.
  3. Connect the Black (Negative) wire to Terminal 2.
  4. Click "Start Simulation" to observe the light.

### Step 2: Breaking the Path

- **Concept**: If the loop is broken, the circuit is "Open," and electricity cannot flow.
- **Activity**:
  1. Delete the Red wire.
  2. Click "Start Simulation" and observe that the light remains off.

### Step 3: The "Magic Bridge" (Push Button)

- **Concept**: A button acts as a bridge that creates a path only when pressed.
- **Activity**:
  1. Place a push button into the circuit.
  2. Use the following wiring configurations to test the button:
     - **Always On**: Connect 1-1 and 1-2 (internally connected).
     - **Working Switch**: Connect 1-1 to the battery and 2-2 to the light bulb.
  3. Push the button during the simulation to "close" the circuit and light the bulb.

## Tips for Success

- **Color Coding**: Use **Red** for Positive (+) and **Black** for Negative (-).
- **Prediction**: Before clicking "Start Simulation," ask the student, "What do you think will happen?"
- **Analogy**: Think of the circuit as a water pipe; the button is a valve that can stop or start the flow.
